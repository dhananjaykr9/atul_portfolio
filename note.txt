# Product Requirements Document (PRD)
**Project:** Professional Academic Portfolio – Dr. Atul M. Gavaskar
**Role:** Assistant Professor, PGTD of English, Gondwana University, Gadchiroli.

## 1. Product Vision
To create a "Digital Academic Identity" that serves as a formal record of teaching, research guidance, and scholarly contributions. The site will act as a bridge between the professor, his MA/PhD students, and the university administration for NAAC/IQAC documentation.

## 2. Target Audience
* **PG Students (MA English):** Accessing syllabus, notes, and SET/NET guidance.
* **PhD Scholars:** Reviewing research areas and supervision availability.
* **Academic Bodies:** University committees and recruiters looking for a verified CV.
* **Literary Community:** Readers of his academic critiques and articles.

## 3. Core Functional Requirements

### A. Professional Identity & Credentials
* **The Academic Banner:** High-visibility display of name and designation: *Assistant Professor, PGTD of English, Gondwana University*.
* **Qualifications Registry:** Dedicated section for **BA, MA (English), SET, PhD**.
* **Digital CV:** A downloadable, print-friendly PDF version of his full academic resume.

### B. Research & Supervision (The "Research Hub")
* **Journal Publications:** A chronological list of research papers published in peer-reviewed/UGC-care listed journals (Title, Journal Name, Year, Link to PDF/Abstract).
* **PhD Guidance Portal:** * Current status of research scholars under his supervision.
    * List of "Specializations" (e.g., American Literature, Indian Diaspora, Linguistics).
    * Information for prospective PhD applicants.
* **Conference Archive:** Record of papers presented at National and International seminars.

### C. Academic Resources (The "Digital Classroom")
* **MA English Repository:** Categorized folders for Semester I-IV (Syllabus, Question Banks, Reading Lists).
* **SET/NET Coaching Corner:** Strategy articles, recommended reading, and PDF notes specifically for English Literature aspirants.
* **Extension Activities:** Records of guest lectures, workshops organized, or departmental responsibilities.

### D. The Literary Blog (The "Scholarly Journal")
* **Content:** Instead of "Books," this serves as his intellectual output. Articles on literary theory, book reviews, and critiques of classic/modern literature.
* **Multilingual Support:** Ability to publish in **English** (for academic work) and **Marathi/Hindi** (for local social/literary outreach).

## 5. Non-Functional Requirements (Technical)

* **Mobile-First Design:** Optimized for 4G/Low-bandwidth areas common in the Gadchiroli region.
* **Indexing/SEO:** The site should appear when searching for "English Professor Gondwana University" or his specific name.
* **Clean Typography:** Using highly readable fonts (e.g., *Merriweather* for serif, *Lato* for sans-serif) to ensure long-form academic articles are easy to read.
* **Minimalist Aesthetic:** Avoiding "flashy" animations; focusing on a "University White & Deep Blue" color palette.

Technical Design Document (TDD)
Project: Academic Portfolio 
Architecture: Jamstack (JavaScript, API, & Markup)

1. Technology Stack
To ensure the site is modern yet extremely fast, I recommend the following:

Frontend: Next.js (React Framework). It allows for Static Site Generation (SSG), meaning pages load almost instantly.

Styling: Tailwind CSS. Provides a clean, professional, academic "look and feel" without bulky CSS files.

Backend/CMS: Sanity.io or Strapi. This allows the Professor to upload research papers and SET notes via a simple "Form" interface without touching code.

Database: PostgreSQL (via Supabase) if tracking "Hits/Views" on papers; otherwise, a Headless CMS is sufficient.

Hosting: Vercel or Netlify. Free tier is usually enough for academic portfolios and includes a Global CDN.

2. System Architecture
The site will follow a decoupled architecture:

Content Layer: All PDFs (Research Papers/Notes) and Blog text are stored in the CMS.

Build Layer: When the Professor adds a new paper, the site automatically "rebuilds" to include the new data.

Delivery Layer: The static files are served via CDN, ensuring the site works well even on 3G/4G mobile networks.

3. Data Models (Schema)
A. Research Paper Schema
title: String

journal_name: String (UGC Care list/Peer-reviewed)

publication_date: Date

doi_link: URL (Optional)

pdf_file: File Upload (Cloudinary/S3)

abstract: Text

B. PhD Scholar Schema
scholar_name: String

research_topic: String

status: Dropdown (Ongoing / Awarded)

registration_year: Number

C. Study Material Schema
category: Dropdown (MA English / SET-NET / Syllabus)

document_title: String

file: File Upload

4. Key Technical Features
SEO & Metadata: Each research paper will have its own unique URL and OpenGraph tags. This ensures that when he shares a paper link, it appears professionally with a preview on WhatsApp or LinkedIn.

PDF Viewer: Integration of react-pdf-viewer or simple browser-native embedding so students can read notes directly on the site without forced downloads.

Search Functionality: A simple "Fuse.js" or "Algolia" search bar to help students find specific SET/NET topics or literary eras (e.g., "Elizabethan Drama").

Responsive Images: Using Next.js Image component to automatically resize the Professor's portrait for mobile devices to save data.

5. Deployment Strategy
Repository: GitHub (Private/Public).

CI/CD: Automatic deployment via Vercel. Every time the Professor updates the CMS, the site updates live.

Domain: Pointing dr[name].com or [name].in to Vercel via CNAME records.

6. Security & Performance
SSL: Let's Encrypt (Automated via Vercel).

Protection: Since there is no "User Login" for students, the site is inherently secure (Read-only for the public).

Performance Goal: Aiming for a Google Lighthouse score of 95+ on Mobile/Desktop.
